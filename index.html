Volunteer Information

The uOttawa Richard L’Abbé Makerspace has an amazing volunteer program which allows members to become more involved in the makerspace. Not only do you get experience with all different platforms available, this program is a great way to meet new people, and work on fun project

Benefits of being a volunteer

  • Gain valuable experience with technology
  • Meet people with similar interests
  • Collaborate on cool new projects
  • Get involved with the Maker network
  • Receive awesome rewards!

Reward program

Volunteering at the makerspace isn’t just fun, we also have put in place a reward program so that we can give back to you.

0 Personalized laser cut acrylic name tag:
When you have completed your training and certification, you will be a pro with our laser cutter. Show off your skills by designing and cutting your own nametag out of acrylic
20 Volunteer T-shirt
Show off your 20 hour mark with an awesome Makerspace Volunteer T-shirt.
30 10% discount on all Makerstore purchases
The makerstore is your one stop shop for all your “Maker” themed projects, from acrylic for the laser cutter to arduino compatible boards. When you have completed 30 hours of volunteer work, we give you a 10% discount on all your Makerstore purchases for the rest of the semester.
40 Electronics kit worth 20$ from the Makerstore
After 40 hours, your makerskills should be honed. Why not show off what you have learned by assembling your own wearable electronics kit. As a thank you for the work you have done, we are giving you a $20 coupon that you can apply on your next order or use the coupon to get a free electronics kit.
60 3D print on our professional quality printer
Have you ever want to print something really complex or maybe just a high quality 3D printed model? After 60 hours of work, we think you deserve a coupon worth $60 that you can use towards a print on our Dimension SST printer.
80 Makerspace hoodie
After having made such a difference we want you to show it off by with more Makerspace swag. After the 80 hour mark, which is 288,000 seconds, you will receive your very own makerspace hoodie.

How to become a volunteer

Get trained:

Become familiar with all the platforms available in the makerspace Check out the resources page for more information on training and certification requirements. 

  • Soldering station
  • Laser cutter
  • 3D printing on all

Get Certified

To get certified, the volunteer must have a standard knowledge of the equipment. Receive training from a Makerspace coordinator and wait up to 24 hrs before returning to perform their certification test.

 Volunteer

Now that you are trained an certified. You are ready to go current opportunities for a list of volunteering jobs.

 


Volunteer Opportunities

  • Welcone newcomers and give tours of the Makerspace
  • Help out during workshops (2-3 times a week)
  • Help out with on campus events such as Design day, makerspace challenges, clubs day, entrepreneurship week
  • Help out with off campus events such as makermobile outings, conferences, festivals
  • Help maintain equipment
  • Provide training for those who wish to learn more about the technology in the Makerspace
  • Create and design projects requested by the community
